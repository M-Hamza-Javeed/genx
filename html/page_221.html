Case Study: Regular Expressions

A kind of speciﬁcation that turns up repeatedly in Unix tools and scripting languages is the regular

expression (‘regexp’ for short).

We consider it here as a declarative minilanguage for describing

text patterns; it is often embedded in other minilanguages.

Regexps are so ubiquitous that the are

hardly thought of as a minilanguage, but they replace what would otherwise be huge volumes of

code implementing different (and incompatible) search capabilities.

This introduction skates over some details like POSIX extensions, back-references, and internation-

alization features; for a more complete treatment, see Mastering Regular Expressions [Friedl].

Regular expressions describe patterns that may either match or fail to match against strings.

simplest regular-expression tool is grep(1), a ﬁlter that passes through to its output every line in its
